Senior Capstone Project Presentation

University of Alaska Anchorage, Spring 2021

Ty Bergstrom & Ian McAndrews

We developed an app for the Alaska Public Health Information Response Team to help monitor and respond to misinformation in social media. The app significantly simplifies their notification process. We also used the app as an opportunity to provide a variety of Machine Learning and Natural Language Processing tools and data visualizations to help their efforts.





The App

Submit Comments

Students who monitor social media for misinformation can easily log in and copy+paste the link to a comment along with the text.

Get Notifications

The response team receives an automatic email with a link that takes them directly to the comment so they can quickly respond.


View Comments

The Response Team members can also log in and quickly view the newest comments. It's more accessible to view which comments they need to prepare responses for, and the links take them directly to the comments.

Security

Only registered users have access to the app, and only the admin account can invite new users. There is no software to download to use the app.


Admin

The admin account has control of managing, inviting, and removing users. It's also easy to add responders to the email-only list, so they don't have to use the app at all. This makes it easier for more members to join the team and be involved.

Users

The student-monitor users have access to the app to submit comments, and responders can also use the app for the data visuals. Responders on the email-only list will not have access.
